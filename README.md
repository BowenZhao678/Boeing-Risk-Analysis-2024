# Financial Risk Analysis of Boeing’s 2024 Safety Incidents

## Project Overview
This project analyzes several Boeing aircraft safety incidents in 2024 and evaluates their operational and financial implications. The analysis focuses on how technical failures and operational breakdowns can propagate into financial risks for a large aerospace manufacturer.

The project also explores how corporate culture and engineering decision-making influence long-term risk exposure.

## Key Events Studied
The analysis focuses on several high-profile incidents involving Boeing aircraft in 2024, including:

- Alaska Airlines Flight 1282 (737-9 MAX) – door-plug panel separation and rapid depressurization  
- Delta Airlines (757) – nose-gear wheel separation before takeoff  
- United Airlines (777-200) – landing-gear tire separation after takeoff  
- Southwest Airlines (737-800) – engine cowling detachment during takeoff  

These incidents illustrate how operational failures can create financial and reputational risks for manufacturers and airlines.

## Risk Categories Analyzed
The project evaluates several categories of risk:

- Operational risk – quality control failures and production disruptions  
- Regulatory risk – FAA investigations and production restrictions  
- Legal and liability risk – airline compensation and potential litigation  
- Financial risk – impacts on cash flow, earnings, and aircraft deliveries  
- Reputational risk – loss of investor and public confidence  

## Financial Impact Channels
The project examines how operational incidents affect financial performance, including:

- delayed aircraft deliveries affecting revenue  
- compensation and remediation costs  
- increased regulatory oversight and production limits  
- declining investor confidence and market pressure  

## Key Insights
The analysis highlights that long-term risk management in aerospace depends heavily on:

- engineering quality and safety standards  
- effective operational controls  
- strong corporate governance  
- corporate culture that prioritizes product safety over short-term financial performance  

Focusing on engineering excellence and safety culture can help reduce long-term operational and financial risks.
